Visualizing State Income Distributions in the United States

Welcome

Real (2018$) annual household income (HHINCOME from IPUMS CPS ASEC) in each state and DC from 1976 to 2018 is displayed on a 3D chart to show how income is distributed across the United States and within states.

Things you can do

  • Apply individual state labels.
  • Choose the year.
  • Display four versions of real household income:
  1. RHH: Real household income in 2018$
  2. ERHH: Real household income per (equivalized) person using square root equivalence scale, see Johnson et al., 2005, p. 13
  3. RHHRPP: Real household income adjusted for state prices using BEA's regional price parities; 2008-2018 only
  4. ERHHRPP: Real household income per (equivalized) person adjusted for state prices, 2008-2018 only
  • Download the data in the chart by clicking the down arrow in the top-right corner of the screen.
  • Resize the chart by resizing your browser window.

The rendering (by amCharts) is interactive. Hover your mouse over different parts of the chart to get a popup with information.

Rendering may take a few seconds (depending on your device and connection).

Go to work

Click the Start link below to start your visualizations.

Return to this page and click the Start link again to create a new tab where you can choose a different year. You can do this as many times as you wish. Now you can easily compare across years by moving among tabs.

The color scheme is always based on real household income (RHH) in 1976. This makes it easy to see how states changed rank over time.

Documentation and Help

For more detail and explanation, please see:

Barreto, H. and Truong, S. (2020), "Visualizing Income Inequality in the United States", Working Papers 2020-3, DePauw University, Department of Economics and Management.

Truong, S. and Barreto, H., "Technical Appendix for Visualizing Income Inequality in the United States", is available from pypi.org/project/incomevis/, which has a licensed Python package for this project. It includes advanced features such as controlling the color scheme and animation.

Details on bootstrapped SEs are in this Excel workbook: 1976RHHINCOME.xlsm.

California and DC median income rankings are in this Excel workbook: ranking.xlsx.
